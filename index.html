Mission FAQs

What is the duration of the Artemis IV mission?

Artemis IV is planned for a 580-day surface mission, with a 9-month transit each way. Total mission duration is approximately 27 months from launch to Earth return.

How is crew health monitored during deep space travel?

Continuous biometric sensors track heart rate, blood oxygen, sleep cycles, and radiation exposure. AI-assisted diagnostics relay data to mission physicians on Earth with a maximum 24-minute signal delay.

What propulsion system powers the Mars transit vehicle?

The transit vehicle uses a nuclear thermal propulsion (NTP) engine paired with solar electric ion thrusters for fine orbital adjustments, reducing transit time by approximately 30% over conventional chemical rockets.

Can civilians apply for future missions?

Yes. The Civilian Astronaut Programme opens applications every two years. Candidates must pass a rigorous physical and psychological screening process, followed by an 18-month training period at the Johnson Space Centre.
